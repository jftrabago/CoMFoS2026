About

This conference focuses on the modeling, numerical analysis, and scientific computing of nonlinear phenomena, covering both theoretical developments and computational approaches. It aims to showcase the latest research in applied mathematics and its practical applications, spanning a broad range of topics related to partial differential equations.

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Modeling and Analysis of Physical, Biological, and Chemical Systems
  • Partial Differential Equations and Applied Analysis
  • Numerical Analysis, Scientific Computing, and Algorithms
  • Optimization, Variational Methods, and Control
  • Inverse Problems, Interface Evolution, and Free Boundary Phenomena
  • Data Science, Artificial Intelligence, and Computational Imaging

Through invited talks and interactive sessions, participants will engage with leading experts, exchange ideas, and gain insights into mathematical frameworks and computational methodologies for addressing complex nonlinear problems across disciplines such as physics, biology, and engineering.

CoMFoS 2026

The conference is organized in conjunction with the 2026 edition of CoMFoS, which was initiated in 1995 under the auspices of the activity group "Continuum Mechanics Focusing on Singularities (CoMFoS)" of the Japan Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics (JSIAM). From April 2010, the activity group CoMFoS was renamed "Mathematical Aspects of Continuum Mechanics (MACM)". The links for the recent CoMFoS meetings can be accessed here: CoMFoS2020, CoMFoS2021, CoMFoS2022, and CoMFoS2023.

The last CoMFoS meeting took place on March 16–17, 2025, in Kanazawa, Ishikawa Prefecture, Japan, in conjunction with MNANP2025, a workshop dedicated to the Modeling and Numerical Analysis of Nonlinear Phenomena.

CoMFoS–NASC 2026 is the result of the active collaboration between Kanazawa University and the University of the Philippines in recent years. This year, the conference is co-organized by the Numerical Analysis and Scientific Computing (NASC) Academic Group of the University of the Philippines Diliman and by the Mathematics Program of the University of the Philippines Cebu.
